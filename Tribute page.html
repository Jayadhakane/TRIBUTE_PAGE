Sindhutai Sapkal

Mother of orphans

The women who saved a many lives

Here's a short time line of Sindhutai Sapkal life:

  • 1948 - Born in Pimpri Meghe village,Wardha district
  • 1968 - She got marry at minor age where she was only ten and the person who was 20 year old thean her.
    • When Sindhutai was 10 years old, she was married to “Srihari Sapkal”, 30 years old. She was the mother of 3 children at the age of 20.
  • 1970 -She also opened her first NGO named Savitribai Phule Girls Hostel which was registered in Chikaldara. She then ran an orphanage named Sanmati Bal Niketan Sanstha – in the Hadapsar area of Pune.
  • 2013 - she was awarded with the Mother Teresa Awards
    • Sindhutai Sapkal arrives at Mother Teresa Memorial International Awards, held at Hotel Leela, in Mumbai, on October 27, 2013.
  • 2017 - she was awarded with the Nari Shakti Puraskar
    • The Nari Shakti Puraskar is an annual award given by the Ministry of Women and Child Development of the Government of India to individual women or to institutions that work towards the cause of women empowerment.
  • 2021 - she was awarded with the one of the highest and honored Award of India Padam shri
  • 2022 - At the age of 73 a great inspiration and social worker Sindhutai Sapkal died

"I had no one with me, everyone abandoned me. I knew the pain of being alone and unwanted. I didn't want anyone to go through the same. And I feel immense pride and pleasure to see some of my children doing so well in their lives. One of my children made a documentary on my life."

-- Indian Social Worker Sindhutai Sapkal
